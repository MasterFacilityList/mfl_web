About the Kenya Master Facility and Community Units Database



The Kenya Master Facility and Community Units Database is a Ministry of Health initiative to avail an online platform for the public to query and locate health facilities and their corresponding community health units in Kenya. A user can search and/or filter by various facility or community health unit attributes.

There is also discoverability through the Facility Geolocator Service. It visualizes administrative units at national, county, constituency and ward levels, which shows facilities in that particular ward. Individual Community Health Units can be viewed on a map on the view showing their particular details.

What is a Health Facility ?

This is a health service delivery structure within a defined geographical area, that has one or more units/ departments operating within it e.g pharmacy unit, laboratory department etc. Each health facility is usually allocated health workers and there is an officer in charge in the facility who are charged with delivering health services to the general public. Within a health facility are Community Health Units which are smaller health service delivery structures compared to health facilities they are associated to.

In order to locate health facilities closest to you the MFL has plotted the location of these health facilities on a map to enable individuals locate the Health Facilities closest to them with the particular services offered.

What is a Community Health Unit ?

This is a health service delivery structure within a defined geographical area covering a population of approximately 5,000 people. Each unit is assigned 2 Community Health Extension Workers (CHEWs) and community health volunteers who offer promotive, preventative and basic curative health services.

Why Community Health Units ?

It is governed by a Community Health Committee (CHC) and each Community Health unit is linked to a specific Health facility.
