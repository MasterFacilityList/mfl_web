About the Kenya Master Health Facility List
Geolocate Facilities
The Facility Geolocator visualizes administrative units at national, county, constituency and ward levels, which shows facilities in that particular ward. Individual Community Health Units can be viewed on a map on the view showing their particular details.

Kenya Master Health Facility List (KMHFL) is an application with all health facilities and community units in Kenya. Each health facility and community unit is identified with unique code and their details describing the geographical location, administrative location, ownership, type and the services offered.

KMHFL provides a platform for the public to rate health facilities and Community Health Unit services. Regulators have linked to KMHFL. Searching Facilities:

To search for a facility click “Find Facilities” - This provides a list of all health facilities and there is a provided advance search where you can filter facilities by administrative location, ownership, type and the services offered

Facility Geo-location:

To search for a facility geolocation click on “Facility Geo-locator” - This displays health facilities in a map

Community Health Units

To search for a community health unit click on “Find Community Health Unit” - This provides a list of all community health units and there is a provided advance search where you can filter community unit by administrative location

What is a Facility?

This is a defined health service delivery structure that provides services and has one or more departments operating within it e.g. Outpatient, pharmacy, laboratory . In KMHFL, a facility is described by it’s unique code, ownership, type, administrative and geographical location, and services provided.

These are governed by a Community Health Committee (CHC) and each Community Health unit is linked to a specific Health facility.
What is a Community Health Unit ?

This is a health service delivery structure within a defined geographical area covering a population of approximately 5,000 people. Each unit is assigned 2 Community Health Extension Workers (CHEWs) and community health volunteers who offer promotive, preventative and basic curative health services.

A user can do the following with regards to Community Health Units:

  • Search for a Community Health Unit
  • View Details of a Community Health Unit
  • Rate a Community Health Unit
  • View a Community Health Unit on map
